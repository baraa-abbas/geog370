In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

Describe in your own words how you displayed the map in different projections using QGIS

In the bottom right hand corner of QGIS, to the left of a comment bubble there will be a globe icon followed by "ESRI:####" or "EPSG:...".
Click on that to change the projection of the shapefile. You just need to search the ESRI number.

WGS84 Projection

Some observations

  • right angle grid lines
  • distorts shape at poles
  • distorts area at poles
  • Greenland not as big as in Web Mercator Projection

Aitoff Projection

Some observations

  • map is not a rectangle
  • shape distorted as you look away from the meridian and equator
  • area looks better conserved near meridian
  • Greenland vs. Africa looks accurate

Web Mercator Projection

Some observations

  • right angle grid
  • shape preserved
  • poles appear bigger

Sphere Winkel I Projection

Some observations

  • not as much area distortion vs. Mercator
  • non-rectangle map
  • shape is distorted a bit along the edges of the map

World Cyclindrical Equal Area Projection

Some observations

  • right angle grid
  • area preserved
  • shape is very distorted away from the equator

World Equidistant Conic Projection

Some observations

  • round map
  • Antarctica is around almost everything
  • Alaska and Russia are far apart

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant Projection

Some observations

  • round map
  • Antarctica encircles the rest of the world
  • Alaska and Russia are close
  • UN map

World Van der Grinten I Projection

Some observations

  • grid curves instead of lines, only equator is straight
  • area distortion toward poles
  • shape distortion away from the meridian and equator

World Polyconic Projection

Some observations

  • only equator and meridian are lines, all other "lines" are very distorted
  • looks like conic projection was applied to both poles
  • Russia and Alaska are apart like in the World Equidistant Conic projection
